Final Year Research Project

Published study

Exploring the Antimicrobial Potential of Allium Sativum and Lawsonia Inermis Emulgels: A Comparative Insight
Arumilli S, Maity SK, Bhandari R, Pati S, Bhandari R, Ghosh A. Int J Pharm Res Applications, Vol 10, Issue 2 (Mar–Apr 2025), pp. 976–986. ISSN 2456-4494.
DOI: 10.35629/4494-1002976986 · Duration: June 2024 – April 2025
Published

This project formulated topical emulgels from garlic (Allium sativum) and henna (Lawsonia inermis) extracts to compare their antimicrobial performance as a plant-based alternative to conventional topical antimicrobials. Extracts were prepared by maceration, incorporated into a Carbopol-based emulgel base, and benchmarked across physicochemical, safety, and antimicrobial parameters.

My contribution: formulation and evaluation work as part of a six-member team in the Department of Pharmaceutics, Aditya College of Pharmacy, including extract preparation, emulgel evaluation testing, and antimicrobial assay support.
